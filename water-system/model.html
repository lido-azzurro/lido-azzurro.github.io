Scientific Model

Model philosophy
We keep models simple enough for interactive exploration while preserving correct cause → effect structure, consistent units, and realistic constraints.

Pumping (fluid + energy balance)

Hydraulic power required to lift water from depth h at volumetric flow Q is:

P = ρ · g · h · Q / η

Where ρ is water density, g gravity, and η pump efficiency (0–1). The simulation enforces operational limits (insufficient power / overload).

Solar power supply

Electrical power from photovoltaic panels is approximated by:

P = G · A · η

Where G is irradiance, A panel area, and η panel efficiency.

Filtration (clarity vs. head loss)

Mechanical filtration is modeled as a turbidity reduction that improves with thickness, while flow decreases due to added resistance. This is a simplified representation of pressure drop through porous media.

Hardness and treatment (chemistry)

Hardness is based on Ca²⁺ and Mg²⁺ expressed as CaCO₃ equivalents. Treatment modules apply simplified removal curves and report side effects (e.g., pH shift, sludge).

These models are intentionally simplified and meant for conceptual understanding and parameter sensitivity.
